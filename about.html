Where We Began

Built on a Ridge,
Not for a Skyline

Mountain Stay opened in 2014 on a quiet ridge above Landour, a few kilometres past the crowds of Mall Road. The land had belonged to a Garhwali family for three generations — an old apple orchard with a view nobody wanted to build over.

We kept the orchard. The hotel was built around it instead of in place of it, with eighteen rooms arranged across three low stone buildings that follow the natural slope of the hill rather than flattening it. Nothing here was built taller than the deodar trees beside it.

What started as a single guesthouse for trekkers passing through has grown, slowly and on purpose, into a small resort — but the instinct to keep it modest, personal, and quiet has never changed.

What Guides Us

Our Philosophy

Three ideas have shaped every decision we've made about this hotel, from the size of the rooms to the way the staff are trained.

Respect the Land

We built around the existing orchard and forest line rather than clearing them — the hill dictated the hotel's shape, not the other way round.

Stay Small

Eighteen rooms is a ceiling we've held to since day one, so every guest is known by name, not by a room number on a screen.

Hire Local, Stay Rooted

Most of our team grew up within a few valleys of here — their knowledge of these hills is the real luxury we offer guests.
